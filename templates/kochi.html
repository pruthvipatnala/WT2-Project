KOCHI

Introduction



It's not for nothing that Kochi, aka Cochin, is called Queen of the Arabian Sea. The Chinese fishing nets floating lazily above the surface of the sea make for a picture-perfect setting and the Western Ghats in the east make for an imposing sentry. Lovingly referred to as the Gateway to Kerala, which is in turn lovingly referred to as God's Own Country, Cochin is another paradise in the state that reminds one of times gone by and offers visitors a laid-back ambience. While there is a lot to be seen and done in Cochin, the very best things you could probably do here are relax and take in the opulent culture of the city and binge on the amazing South Indian food. Cochin reflects the influences of several kingdoms that have played a major role in the creation of modern-day Kochi, namely Portuguese, Chinese, Dutch, Arab and British. The architecture and culture speak volumes of all these dynasties which make Cochin a must-visit destination for art and history lovers. Not to mention, Cochin has also been named as one of the top 3 tourist destinations by the World Travel and Tourism Council. If you are planning a trip to this city any time soon, make sure you book one of the lovely hotels in Cochin well in advance. Following is a quick guide to the countless sights and sounds of this grand old city that a visitor must explore, and a few facts to make your trip hassle-free.



Know Your Place



Local Languages: : Malayalam & English

Best Time to Visit: October to February during the winter season when the weather is nippy but pleasant; start of March is a good time to visit too, when it starts to get warm but isn't terribly hot.

Clothing: If you are visiting during summer, carry only light cotton clothes if you want a pleasant holiday. During winter, carry along a few woollen clothes or light jackets as the temperature can drop a little.

Moving around: Moving within Cochin is inexpensive and easy. There are privately owned as well as state-run bus services in the city. Rickshaws and taxis are available frequently on hire throughout the city.

Climate -

Summer: March to June, Warm and humid Temperatures – Can go up to 38°C
Monsoon: July to September, Heavy rainfall
Winter: October to February, Cool and pleasant Temperatures – 16°C to 33°C

Places to Visit



Mattancherry Palace: Also known as the Dutch Palace, Mattancherry Palace is reminiscent of the erstwhile Portuguese style of architecture. Constructed in the year 1557, this palace was presented to the then king of Kerala, Veera Kerala Varma. After renovations by the later kings of Kerala, the Mattancherry Palace has taken on a distinctive Indian architectural style.

Fort Kochi: If you are fascinated by the history of Cochin, then one place you must definitely visit is Fort Kochi. This small town was given to the king of Kochi as a mark of gratitude by the Portuguese in the year 1503 and bears a Portuguese, British and Dutch resemblance where architecture is concerned. Some important places you must visit in Fort Kochi are Thakur House, David Hall, Fort Emmanuel, St. Francis Church, etc.

Jew Town: Jew Town was an area granted to the Jews when they took refuge in 1524. You must visit this part of the city just for the old-world charm of it, and of course, a number of souvenirs that you can take back home. Check out the antique and curio shops selling jewellery, wooden furniture, lamps, vintage photographs and tons of other vintage paraphernalia.

Marine Drive: Just like Mumbai, Kochi too has a charming promenade. Marine Drive is visited by tons of people everyday, which stretches from Jankar Jetty to Ernakulam Boat Jetty. There are also quite a few tourist attractions here that you can check out, like China Net Bridge, Rainbow Bridge, etc.

Cherai beach: One of the loveliest beaches in India, Cherai beach is located near Vypeen Island. Fondly referred to as the "Princess of the Arabian Sea", Cherai beach is a lone stretch of clean and shallow water that is ideal for swimming and photo-ops. Plus, the backdrop of lovely backwaters and Chinese fishing nets make the whole area absolutely picturesque.

Willingdon Island: This island connects Kochi with seaports around the world. Named after Lord Willingdon, Willingdon Island was developed in the year 1936. This is quite a major trade centre for Cochin and it is also home to the Port of Cochin and the customs offices. Also, you will find some wonderful Cochin hotels around this area, so make sure to check those out for your holiday!
